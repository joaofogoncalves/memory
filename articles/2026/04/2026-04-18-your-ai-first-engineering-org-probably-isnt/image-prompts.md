# Image Prompts

## Hero Image

A wide editorial painting of a vast machine shop interior at dawn — turn-of-the-century engineering firm reimagined with contemporary precision. Rows of lathes, mills, jigs, and fixtures sit in perfect alignment, interconnected by a network of leather drive belts, shafts, and pulleys running along the ceiling. Everything is running — belts turning, spindles spinning, parts being machined mid-cut — but not a single human figure is visible. Cool morning light floods the scene from tall east-facing windows, casting long soft shadows across the polished floor. In the foreground, a worn wooden desk: a drafting lamp is on, a notebook lies open with clean technical drawings and handwritten margin notes, a half-drunk cup of coffee beside it. The scene hums with controlled, deliberate motion — a system built by someone who is now elsewhere, thinking. Painterly brushwork, visible texture, warm wood and brass tones against cool steel-blue morning light, slight cinematic grain, shallow depth of field. No text, no icons, no UI elements, no modern screens. The feeling: the workshop is the worker. Reference: editorial illustration for The New York Times Magazine or Wired long-read opener — atmospheric, not decorative.

## Team Shape: Before vs After

A single horizontal editorial illustration showing two team arrangements side by side, separated by a thin vertical divider. The tone is graphic and refined — not a boxes-and-arrows diagram and not a painterly scene, but somewhere in between: stylized silhouettes on a flat, moody background with subtle paper-grain texture.

Left half ("Before"): A traditional engineering org. Roughly ten simplified human silhouettes arranged in a tight pyramid — one figure at the top (manager), three in a row beneath (senior engineers), six clustered below (individual contributors). All figures identical, slightly abstracted, in muted grayscale. Thin pale lines connect them in a hierarchy tree. The composition feels dense, orderly, and static — every figure facing forward, no motion. Small label at the bottom of this half: **Before**. Optional subtitle beneath: *Hierarchy, headcount, hand-offs.*

Right half ("After"): A different shape entirely. One silhouette stands slightly apart and elevated (the architect, distinguished by posture — looking sideways at a drafting surface, not forward). Three or four smaller silhouettes occupy a loose horizontal row in the middle distance (the operators), each engaged in a different gesture — pointing, reviewing, gesturing at something unseen. Around and between the human figures, a diffuse cloud of small glowing teal points and soft connecting lines drifts through the air (the agents) — suggestive of fireflies or drafting light, not menacing, not mechanical. The composition feels loose, horizontal, more open space than occupied space. Small label at the bottom: **After**. Optional subtitle: *One architect, a few operators, a swarm of agents.*

Palette: deep dark navy background (#0e131e), muted grayscale human silhouettes, teal (#44d8f1) accents used only for the agent cloud, connecting lines, and small highlight details. Subtle paper-grain or film-grain texture throughout. Editorial, quiet, confident. No corporate polish, no gradient shine, no 3D depth effects. The feeling: two constellations of the same organization, separated by a transformation — the team didn't shrink, it changed shape.

Format: 1800x900px (2:1) · webp

Target path: `media/team-shape.webp`
