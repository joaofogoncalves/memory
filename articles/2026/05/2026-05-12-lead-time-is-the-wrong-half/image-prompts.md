## Hero Image — Control room

Photo-realistic wide-angle photograph of an empty industrial control room interior at night. A long curved console fills the frame: banks of analog meters, dials, indicator lights, and CRT-style monitors. One central screen glows soft teal in the otherwise cold-blue ambient room, its content indistinct — the suggestion of a single live metric rather than legible text. An empty operator chair sits pushed back from the desk, slightly turned. Concrete floor with worn vinyl tiles, exposed conduit on the ceiling, faint dust haze catching the light from the lit screen. The atmosphere is quiet pre-dawn vigilance. Composition is centered with the lit console as the focal point, generous breathing room above and on both sides so thumbnails can crop cleanly. No people, no signage, no readable text or branding. Shot on 35mm wide-angle, sharp focus through the depth of field, slight bokeh on the back wall. Style: documentary interior photography in the register of 1970s NASA mission control or Wim Wenders cinematography, slightly desaturated, muted cool palette with one warm teal accent on the active screen, matte finish, no glossy reflections, no painterly cues, no illustrated elements.
