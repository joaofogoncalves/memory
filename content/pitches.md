## Talk abstract (200–300 words)

**Title:** The Filter Is Already Running: What Survives the AI Coding Era

In March, AI-assisted code knocked Amazon's order volume down 99% for six hours. A 2025 survey found 43% of AI-generated code changes need debugging in production. Most companies will read these as cautionary tales. They're not. They're a sorting mechanism. The teams shipping faster than ever in 2026 aren't the ones using AI more carefully. They're the ones using it harder, with their hands on the wheel. The depth premium compounds in the agent era. The shallow-knowledge engineer's loop runs in reverse.

This talk walks through the pattern from inside an engineering function that operates a 14-agent AI orchestration system in production. We'll cover the verification asymmetry that decides who ships clean code and who ships theatrical tests. The architectural choice between AI as autocomplete and AI as a teammate, and why most teams are still building for the wrong one. A specific failure mode I've seen in production at AI speed: the model simplified the wrong thing, the test suite passed, the retry logic disappeared. Junior caught nothing. Senior caught it in thirty seconds. Same code. Different careers.

**Takeaways:**

- Why "AI fluency" is the wrong proxy for skill, and what to measure instead.
- The verification asymmetry: how to catch what the test suite can't.
- A working pattern for splitting human and agent work in code review at machine speed.

João Gonçalves is a Founding Engineer at BRIDGE IN, where he runs a 14-agent AI orchestration system. Previously Director of Software Engineering at Altium following the $20M acquisition of Valispace, where as Head of Technology he shipped AI features into the product within a month of ChatGPT's launch and as Head of DevOps cut release time 88% before that.
