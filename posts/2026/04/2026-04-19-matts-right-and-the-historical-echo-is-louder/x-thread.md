**Tweet 1 (hook):**
There's a take going around that AI makes "adequate" work free. The adequate worker is the one in trouble, not the bad one.

It's right. And the historical parallel is sharper than most people are reading it.

---

**Tweet 2:**
Same thing played out in the 80s and 90s with computers. The adequate worker who kept doing the job the old way wasn't fired in year one. Or year three.

They got absorbed, moved sideways, buffered by colleagues picking up the slack.

Then the buffer ran out.

---

**Tweet 3:**
That transition took twenty years. This one is compressing into two.

Tech transformations don't punish adequacy gradually anymore. They punish it in quarters.

---

**Tweet 4:**
You can't wait for your company to train you into it.

The people who came out ahead last time weren't the most talented. They were the ones experimenting on their own time, before it became a mandate.

Back then the window was years. Now it's quarters.

---

**Tweet 5 (landing):**
Adequate is a stable position when the ground isn't moving. The ground is moving fast.

The skill isn't "using AI." It's being the kind of person who rebuilds how they work when the tools change.

You used to have a decade. Now you have a year.
