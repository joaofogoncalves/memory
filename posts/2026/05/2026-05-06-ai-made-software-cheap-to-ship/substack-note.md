AI made software cheap to ship. The hard part is now figuring out what to ship.

That gap is also the reason we walked into Agents Day with no team, no plan, no idea what we were building.

We spent the first hour on the question. Picked Cloudflare's challenge as the constraint, gave an agent access to our GitHub accounts, and from our skills it produced a plan with roles and tasks. By hour two we were building.

We called it Quorum. A chat-native agent that lives in your team's group chat and walks ideas through ideation, validation, and planning, with backflow when constraints shift. AI made software cheap; picking what to build is the new bottleneck; getting it wrong now costs more than ever because everything else got faster.

Built end-to-end on Cloudflare. Deployed and user-tested by the end of the day.

Most of the typing was the agents. None of us could have shipped this solo in a day.

The less obvious part was the coordination. Three people in parallel usually collide somewhere. We didn't, because the agents knew what each other were building. We never had to pass implementation details around the table. The plan was shared context, and the agents read it before the humans did.

The layer AI doesn't cover yet is the what. We built that. AI then collapsed the how into half a day. And the coordination layer mostly disappeared because the agents had a shared head.

Same room. Three people. The bottleneck moved twice in one day.
